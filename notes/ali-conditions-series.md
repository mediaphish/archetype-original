## Definition of done — by layer

### Journal (primary visible chapter)

“Done” for the series means:

- **Seven published journal entries** (one URL per condition above), each with front matter that matches project expectations: title, slug, summary, `publish_date`, categories, and structured takeaways where used.
- Each post **owns one condition**: its job is to explain that pattern in plain language and tie it to lived leadership—not to re-litigate the other six in depth.
- **Proof is files + refreshed site index**, not chat confirmation: source markdown lives under `ao-knowledge-hq-kit/journal/`, the knowledge step has been run so the content list updates, and changes are saved to the project in the usual way.

### Doctrine / measurement language

“Done” means the language in the posts **matches** the public ALI framing (the seven patterns as behavioral conditions, Drift as early warning—not an eighth “virtue”), and does not contradict:

- `database/ALI_SURVEY_FOUNDATION.md`
- Culture Science Section 7 corpus, especially `Culture_Science_SECTION_7_PART_4.md` (the seven categories)

### Knowledge / Archy / FAQs

For this first shipped bundle:

- **Corpus anchors** are documented in `notes/ali-conditions-corpus-map.md` so drafting stays grounded.
- **FAQ edits** are optional for each condition; default for v1 is **no new FAQ files** unless explicitly chosen later.
- **Archy alignment**: no separate corpus edits were required beyond existing Section 7 sources; future FAQ or corpus edits would count as their own checklist items.

### Marketing / ALI page

Default for v1: **no copy changes** to `src/pages/cultureScience/ALI.jsx`; the page already reflects the seven conditions. Any future tweak to that page is its own small deliverable with its own approval.

## Overlap rules

1. **One spine per post**: Each chapter answers “what does this *one* condition mean for people day to day?” Avoid repeating another condition’s core thesis (e.g. don’t make the Clarity chapter mostly about trust repair—that belongs under Trust).
2. **Cross-links, not repeats**: It’s fine to mention another condition in passing (“when clarity fades, drift shows up”) but the **deep dive** stays on the title topic.
3. **Same scripture / story**: Do not reuse identical anecdotes or long biblical blocks across two posts in this series. If two posts need the same idea, phrase it freshly or pick a second illustration.
4. **Contrast with devotionals**: These seven are **culture-science / ALI doctrine** chapters, not devotionals. No `scripture_reference` slot required unless you later choose to add one deliberately.

---

## Voice bar (editorial guardrails)

- **Tone**: First-person capable, grounded, non-corporate. Prefer concrete scenes (meetings, feedback, pressure) over abstract slogans.
- **Audience**: Leaders of small and mid-sized organizations who carry real weight—same spirit as the Culture Science / servant-leadership journal line.
- **ALI naming**: Call the patterns **conditions** or **patterns** in plain language; align with the ALI page one-line definitions (what “good” looks like vs what erosion looks like).
- **Drift**: Treat as **early warning** and compound slips—not as a moral failing list. Match the site: small tolerated slips, not sudden catastrophe.
