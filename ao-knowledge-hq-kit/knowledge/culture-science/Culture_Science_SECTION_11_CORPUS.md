SECTION 11 — The Boundaries of Culture Science  
What It Explains — and What It Explicitly Refuses to Do

Culture Science is powerful precisely because it is constrained.

This section defines the boundaries of the discipline — not as limitations, but as protections.  
Without clear boundaries, any system that explains leadership environments becomes dangerous.

Culture Science does not exist to control leaders, manage people, or prescribe behavior.  
It exists to make environments legible so responsibility can be exercised with clarity.

---

I. Culture Science Explains Conditions, Not People

Culture Science does not explain:
- who is good or bad  
- who is right or wrong  
- who is trustworthy or untrustworthy  
- who should be promoted or removed  
- who is the “problem”  

It explains:
- the conditions people are operating inside  
- the pressures shaping behavior  
- the structures amplifying or suppressing leadership  
- the patterns that emerge under stress  

This boundary is non-negotiable.

---

II. Culture Science Does Not Diagnose Motives or Intentions

Intentions are invisible.  
Motives are inferred.  

Culture Science refuses both.

Its focus is not why someone meant something,  
but what conditions made the outcome predictable regardless of intent.

---

III. Culture Science Does Not Replace Leadership Judgment

Culture Science informs judgment — it does not replace it.

Leaders remain accountable for:
- decisions  
- priorities  
- tradeoffs  
- consequences  

Clarity does not remove responsibility.  
It removes excuses.

---

IV. Culture Science Does Not Create Change by Itself

Understanding is not transformation.

Culture Science removes ambiguity.  
Leaders still do the work.

If leaders do not change behaviors, structures, or systems,  
no amount of clarity will produce a different culture.

---

V. Culture Science Refuses to Be Weaponized

Any attempt to use Culture Science for:
- blame  
- ego protection  
- political leverage  
- coercion  
- punishment  

violates the discipline.

When clarity is used as control, culture collapses.

---

VI. Why These Boundaries Matter

Without boundaries, clarity becomes manipulation.  
With boundaries, responsibility becomes possible.

Culture Science does not tell leaders what to do.  
It shows them what they are responsible for.

---

Canon Lock

Culture Science explains leadership environments — not people, motives, or outcomes —  
so responsibility can be exercised without distortion, coercion, or excuse.
