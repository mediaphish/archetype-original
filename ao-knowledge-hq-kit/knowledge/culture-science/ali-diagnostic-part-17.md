3. EARLY WARNING THRESHOLDS
ALI uses three thresholds:
Level 1  Mild Drift (Observation)A pattern is forming.
Level 2  Moderate Drift (Attention)A pattern is affecting the team. Action recommended.
Level 3  Severe Drift (Intervention)A pattern is harming performance, trust, or safety.
These are not judgments.They are guardrails  the same guardrails leaders build for teams.

4. HOW DRIFT BECOMES VISIBLE WITH ONLY 10 QUESTIONS
The power of ALI is not the number of questions.Its the precision of the questions and the pattern of answers across time.
Each quarters 10-question set:
		tests a different angle of the same dimensions
		reveals subtle shifts
		avoids desensitization
		builds a multi-angle portrait
		surfaces contradictions
Three overlapping questions per quarter serve as statistical anchors.
After one year, leaders see:
		how drift forms
		how it resolves
		what stabilizes teams
		what destabilizes them
		which interventions worked
		which patterns repeat
		what the next quarter will likely look like
Its not 40 questions its 40 angles on the same truth.

5. THE REAL VALUE OF EARLY WARNING SYSTEMS (ESPECIALLY IN SMALL BUSINESS)
Small and mid-sized companies dont break because of spreadsheets.They break because:
		no one noticed the shift
		warning signs were ignored
		conversations were postponed
		culture eroded quietly
		people hid problems
		leaders misread the room
		someone burned out
		someone quit
		someone stopped being honest
ALI stops this cycle.
Not with shame.Not with hierarchy.Not with comparison.
But with something leaders have never had:
the ability to see themselves clearly, early enough to change the outcome.

6. HOW LEADERS USE EARLY WARNINGS
Every ALI early warning comes with:
		what shifted
		why it matters
		what it looks like in behavior
		the likely outcome if ignored
		recommended micro-practices
		optional remediation steps
		reflection prompts
		language to use with the team
It becomes impossible to say:
I didnt know.
It becomes easy to say:
I see it now  and heres what Im doing next.

**SECTION 7  PART XXI
The Dashboard Structure(How Leaders See Their Leadership Reality Inside ALI)**
ALI does not exist to produce data.It exists to produce clarity  the kind leaders can act on without confusion, shame, or guesswork.
The dashboard is the way leaders see that clarity.
It is not a scoreboard.Not a heatmap.Not a ranking.Not a your-team-says-youre-bad wall.
The ALI dashboard is a leadership mirror  simple, stable, and human-first.
Everything inside it answers one question:
What is my leadership creating?

1. THE SIX-PANEL CORE  THE HEART OF THE DASHBOARD
Every leader has a six-panel structure representing the six dimensions ALI measures:
	1	Clarity
	2	Trust
	3	Communication
	4	Consistency
	5	Safety
	6	Emotional Tone
Each panel includes:
		the current quarters score
		direction of movement compared to previous quarter
		the degree of drift (none / mild / moderate / strong)
		the top signal driving that shift
		recommended micro-practice for this quarter
		optional deeper resources or guidance
The purpose of this six-panel core:
 Leaders see their reality in one glance  without needing interpretation.

2. THE LEADERSHIP PORTRAIT  HOW YOU LEAD OVER TIME
This is the stabilizing backbone of ALI.
Instead of showing your score this quarter, the dashboard shows:
		your patterns
		your strengths
		your stability
		your drift tendencies
		your leadership wake
The Leadership Portrait is made of:
2A. Stability Index
Shows how consistent your leadership has been over the last 48 quarters.
2B. Drift Map
Shows where drift tends to emerge first:
		clarity drift
		communication compression
		emotional volatility
		consistency wobble
		safety decline
Drift is not moral.Drift is predictable.And predictable means preventable.
2C. Strength Anchors
Shows your highest-scoring, most stable dimensions.
Serves as the foundation for remediation steps.
2D. Stress Response Pattern
Every leader has one:
		withdraw
		over-function
		dominate
		avoid
		accelerate
		create urgency
		compress communication
ALI maps it clearly without judgment.

3. TEAM EXPERIENCE MAP  THE HOW THEY EXPERIENCE YOU PANEL
This is the team-focused counterpart to the Leadership Portrait.
It includes:
		the top three strengths the team consistently experiences
		the top three friction points the team consistently feels
		a clarity score (how well expectations are understood)
		a trust score (how safe people feel speaking truth)
		a communication score (how clearly information flows)
		a consistency score (how predictable leadership is)
		a safety score (how safe it is to be honest)
		an emotional tone score (how leadership presence impacts the room)
This is not a weapon.This is a translation layer that helps leaders understand the gap between intention and experience.

4. EARLY WARNING PANEL  WHATS STARTING TO SHIFT
This panel synthesizes the Early Warning System (Part XX).
It includes:
		the top drift signal this quarter
		severity (mild, moderate, strong)
		the likely impact if ignored
		the predicted trajectory
		micro-practices to stabilize it
		a 30-second explanation leaders can use with their team
		optional deeper actions leaders can take
This panel is what makes ALI preventative rather than reactive.

5. REMEDIATION PANEL  WHAT TO DO NEXT
This panel uses the remediation pathways (Part XIX and XVIII).
It shows:
5A. Immediate Micro-Practices
Small actions leaders can implement this week.
Examples:
		Ask one clarifying question before assuming.
		Close every meeting with a recap.
		State expectations in one sentence.
		Use one you-first interaction per day.
5B. Leadership Pathways
For moderate issues.
Examples:
		communication reset
		clarity restore
		expectations alignment
		delegation rhythm improvement
		emotional tone regulation
		production/leadership load rebalance
5C. Stabilization Routines
Simple weekly practices to maintain stability.
5D. Optional Deep Work
For teams or leaders needing heavier change.
This panel is where ALI becomes action, not theory.

6. COMPANY PROFILE PANEL  THE SYSTEM YOURE LEADING
ALI maps the organizations pattern (from Part XIX):
		Sensitive System
		Overextended System
		Bottleneck System
		High-Variance System
		Fragmented System
		Underdeveloped System
Each includes:
		how the system behaves
		why it behaves that way
		how leadership stress affects it
		signals that its improving
		signals that its worsening
		recommended system-level interventions
Company patterns make sense of the leadership patterns.

7. BENCHMARK PANEL (INTERNAL ONLY)
No cross-company comparison.No external ranking.No top 10% nonsense.
Just:
		comparison to your own previous quarters
		comparison to other leaders inside your company
		optional anonymized view of leadership stability trends
This avoids ego-driven comparison and keeps the system human-first.

8. ARCHY INTEGRATION PANEL  ASK FOR CLARITY
A small but critical component:
Leaders can open a contextual version of Archy that can explain:
		what their score means
		why a drift signal matters
		how to apply a micro-practice
		what a remediation pathway looks like
		how to talk to the team about the changes
		how to prepare for the next quarter
This creates self-service stability for leaders between surveys.

9. THE CEO/OWNER PANEL  THE STATE OF THE SYSTEM
This is visible only to the top leader(s).
It includes:
		organization-wide drift trends
		department-level stability
		rising signals
		risk clusters
		patterns of improvement
		patterns of decline
		leadership bench strength
		cultural health trajectory
This is the macro version of ALI.

10. ANNUAL MAP PANEL  THE YEAR IN REVIEW
At the end of each year, ALI generates:
		a 12-month drift graph
		a 12-month clarity map
		the most stabilized quarter
		the most unstable quarter
		the most improved dimension
		the dimension that needs attention next
		an annual leadership portrait
		next-year leadership recommendations
		notes from the owner/CEO panel
		a 60-second summary that can be shared with the team
This becomes the cultural equivalent of an annual report.
