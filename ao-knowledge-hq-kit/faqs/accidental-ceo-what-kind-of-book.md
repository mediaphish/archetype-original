It's a leadership memoir, but not the kind where everything works out and the lessons are tidy.

Accidental CEO is a firsthand account of what it actually feels like to lead through pressure, growth, cultural strain, and the weight of responsibility that shows up long before you feel ready for it. The situations were real. The costs were real. The lessons came from living through them, not studying them.

There are practical principles in it you can apply immediately. But the reason people tell me it resonates is that it doesn't pretend leadership is clean.
