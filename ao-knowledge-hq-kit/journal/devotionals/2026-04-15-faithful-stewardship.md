## Scripture

**Matthew 25:14–19**

---

## Reflection

Jesus describes leadership as entrusted responsibility with eventual accountability.

Each servant receives something to manage. The difference is not in what they are given, but in how they respond to it.

Servant leadership is measured by faithfulness with what has been entrusted.

The faithful servants act with initiative. They do not wait for perfect clarity or external pressure. They engage what has been given and move it forward.

The third servant operates from caution and fear. He preserves what was given but does not steward it.

Jesus draws a clear distinction. Responsibility is not fulfilled by avoiding loss. It is fulfilled through faithful engagement.

Leadership requires movement. It requires participation in what has been entrusted.

---

## Practical Application

- Identify what has been entrusted to you right now.
- Take one step to actively steward it.
- Avoid holding back out of hesitation.

---

## Takeaways

- Leadership involves stewardship with accountability.
- Faithfulness requires active engagement.

---

## Closing Thought

What you have been given is meant to be stewarded, not preserved.
