## Scripture

**Hebrews 12:14–15 (ESV)**  
https://www.esv.org/Hebrews+12:14-15/

## Reflection

Hebrews warns about the root of bitterness springing up and causing trouble. Endurance without spiritual vigilance can harden a leader over time.

Pressure alone does not make leaders cynical. Unresolved bitterness does. When disappointment is left untended, it spreads beneath the surface.

Steadfast leadership is not merely about continuing forward. It is about continuing forward without corrosion of the heart.

Peace and holiness require pursuit. Stability is preserved when leaders deal with bitterness before it becomes structural.

## Practical Application

Identify any unresolved resentment.

Address it prayerfully and honestly.

Refuse to let endurance turn into hardness.

## Takeaways

Bitterness erodes steady leadership.

Vigilance preserves internal stability.

## Closing Thought

Today I will endure without allowing bitterness to take root.
