## Scripture

**Matthew 27:59–60**

## Reflection

There is a period where nothing appears to be happening.

The work has been completed, but the outcome is not yet visible.

This space is difficult for leaders. It feels unresolved. It feels uncertain. It invites second-guessing.

The disciples do not yet understand what is coming.

Servant leadership must learn to remain steady in this space.

Not every season produces visible progress. Some require waiting without clarity.

This is not inactivity. It is faithfulness without immediate confirmation.

The silence does not mean the mission has failed.

## Practical Application

- Recognize a season where results are not yet visible.
- Resist the urge to force movement.
- Remain steady in what you know is true.

## Takeaways

- Not every season produces immediate clarity.
- Faithfulness includes waiting without visible progress.

## Closing Thought

Leadership remains steady even when nothing seems to be happening.
