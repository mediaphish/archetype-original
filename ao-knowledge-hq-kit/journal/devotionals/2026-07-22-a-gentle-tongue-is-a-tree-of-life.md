## Scripture

**Proverbs 15:4 (ESV)**

## Reflection

A tree of life is not a small image. It carries the weight of something that sustains, that produces fruit over time, that provides what is needed for living things to grow. Proverbs uses that image for the gentle tongue. Not just pleasant speech. Not just inoffensive language. Something that actually sustains life in the people around it.

The opposite is equally specific. Perverseness breaks the spirit. Not just discourages. Breaks. A leader whose communication is twisted, dishonest, cutting, or manipulative does not merely fail to help. They do active damage to something as fundamental as a person's spirit.

Leaders underestimate this in both directions. They underestimate what gentle, consistent, truthful speech produces over time in the people they lead. And they underestimate what perverse speech costs, especially when it becomes the dominant pattern. A team led by someone whose words regularly break rather than sustain will eventually show it, in disengagement, in fear, in the quiet exodus of people who found somewhere better to grow.

The tree of life image implies that this kind of communication is not a one-time act. Trees grow slowly. The fruit of a gentle tongue accumulates over months and years of consistent, honest, caring speech that treats people as worth the effort of being spoken to well.

## Practical Application

- Identify whether your communication is more consistently producing life or breaking spirit in the people around you.
- Notice where perverseness, cutting remarks, or dishonest framing has become a pattern.
- Choose one relationship this week where you invest in the kind of speech that sustains rather than damages.

## Takeaways

- A gentle tongue produces life in the people around it over time. The fruit accumulates slowly.
- Perverse speech does not merely fail to help. It breaks something as fundamental as a person's spirit.

## Closing Thought

The leader whose words consistently sustain will build something that the one whose words consistently cut never can.
