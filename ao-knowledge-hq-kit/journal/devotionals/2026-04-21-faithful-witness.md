## Scripture

**Matthew 27:11**

---

## Reflection

Jesus speaks when it is necessary, and only then.

After remaining silent under accusation, He answers a direct question of identity with clarity.

Servant leadership is not silent in every situation. It is discerning.

There are moments to refrain from speaking. There are moments to speak with precision.

Jesus does not over-explain. He does not defend beyond what is required.

He states what is true.

Leadership requires this kind of clarity. Not every moment calls for a response, but some moments require a clear and direct one.

Knowing the difference is part of mature leadership.

---

## Practical Application

- Identify where clarity is required.
- Speak directly without over-explaining.
- Align your words with what is true.

---

## Takeaways

- Discernment determines when to speak and when to remain silent.
- Clarity strengthens leadership.

---

## Closing Thought

Strong leaders speak when it is necessary and remain steady when it is not.
