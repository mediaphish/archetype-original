## Scripture

**Proverbs 16:23–24 (ESV)**

## Reflection

Verse twenty-three locates the source of good speech before it describes its effects. The heart of the wise makes the speech judicious. The quality of what comes out of the mouth is determined by the quality of what is happening inside the person speaking.

Judicious speech is not calculated speech. It is speech that has been shaped by wisdom operating in the heart, producing words that are considered, appropriate, and oriented toward what the moment and the person actually need. That process is invisible from the outside. The result is not.

Persuasiveness follows from this naturally. A leader does not need to develop persuasion as a separate skill when the heart is wise and the speech flows from it. People are drawn to words that carry genuine wisdom. They are moved by leaders whose communication reflects something real underneath, not technique applied on top of emptiness.

Verse twenty-four then describes what that speech produces. Sweetness and health. The fruit of wise speech is not just cognitive. It reaches the soul and the body of the person who receives it.

For leaders, this closes the loop on a month's worth of communication content. The ultimate source of speech that produces life is not better technique or more careful word choice. It is a heart that has been made wise. Everything else flows from that.

## Practical Application

- Assess whether the persuasiveness you carry comes from wisdom or from technique.
- Invest in the condition of your heart as the primary communication development you can do.
- Notice this week where your speech reflects genuine wisdom versus where it reflects unexamined habit or reaction.

## Takeaways

- Judicious speech flows from a wise heart. It cannot be reliably produced by technique applied on top of an unformed interior.
- A leader whose heart is wise will find their words carry natural persuasiveness that no skill training alone produces.

## Closing Thought

Develop the heart and the speech follows. Develop only the speech and the heart will eventually betray it.
