## Scripture

**Galatians 5:22–25 (ESV)**  
https://www.esv.org/Galatians+5:22-25/

## Reflection

Paul describes the fruit of the Spirit not as personality traits, but as evidence of transformation. Fruit is not manufactured. It grows from what is cultivated.

In leadership, fruit becomes diagnostic. Love steadies tone. Peace steadies pressure. Patience steadies expectations. Self-control steadies reactions. These are not decorative qualities. They are structural.

When fruit is absent, instability appears. When fruit is present, teams experience consistency. The measure of steadiness is not how intense a leader feels, but how consistent their character remains under strain.

Steadfast leadership is not sustained by effort alone. It is sustained by formation. The fruit reveals the root.

## Practical Application

- Identify one fruit that has felt thin recently.
- Take one action today aligned with cultivating it.
- Invite God to deepen that formation in you.

## Takeaways

- Fruit reveals formation.
- Steady character produces steady leadership.

## Closing Thought

Today I will lead from formed character, not reactive impulse.
