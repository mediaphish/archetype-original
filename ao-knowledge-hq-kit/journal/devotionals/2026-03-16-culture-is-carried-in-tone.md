## Scripture

**Colossians 4:5–6 (ESV)**  
https://www.esv.org/Colossians+4:5-6/

## Reflection

Colossians instructs leaders to walk in wisdom and let their speech be gracious, seasoned with salt. This is not merely about avoiding harsh words. It is about carrying culture in the way we communicate.

Tone establishes atmosphere. Over time, it teaches people what to expect. If speech is consistently impatient, tension becomes normal. If speech is consistently gracious and clear, stability becomes normal.

This is not the same as being slow to speak. It is about what your speech builds over time. Words accumulate. They either construct trust or erode it.

Steadfast leaders recognize that communication is structural. It forms the invisible architecture of the team. Culture is not declared in values statements. It is carried in daily tone.

## Practical Application

- Reflect on the tone most common in your recent conversations.
- Adjust toward clarity and grace.
- Speak in a way that reinforces the culture you intend to build.

## Takeaways

- Tone shapes culture.
- Speech builds invisible structure over time.

## Closing Thought

Today I will speak in a way that strengthens the culture around me.
