## Scripture

**2 Timothy 2:24–25 (ESV)**

## Reflection

Paul is not describing a communication style. He is describing a character requirement for anyone who leads and corrects others.

Not quarrelsome. Kind to everyone. Patient. Gentle in correction. These are not soft ideals attached to a hard job. They are the non-negotiable posture of a leader whose correction is actually meant to produce something rather than simply express displeasure.

A quarrelsome leader can be right about the content of the correction and still produce nothing useful. The manner undermines the message. People defend themselves rather than receive what was said. The issue gets relitigated rather than resolved. The leader wins the argument and loses the outcome.

Gentleness in correction is not weakness. It is the posture that keeps the door open. Verse twenty-five names what is actually at stake. The possibility of repentance, of genuine change, of a person arriving at the truth. That outcome requires a corridor the person can actually walk through. Harshness closes it. Gentleness keeps it open.

A leader who corrects with gentleness is not less serious about the correction. They are more serious about the outcome.

## Practical Application

- Identify a correction you need to deliver and assess whether your current posture is quarrelsome or gentle.
- Choose the manner of delivery that keeps the door open rather than the one that simply expresses what you feel.
- Hold the outcome, not just the content, as the standard for whether the correction was effective.

## Takeaways

- A quarrelsome leader can be right and still produce nothing. The manner undermines the message.
- Gentleness in correction keeps the door open for the outcome correction is actually meant to produce.

## Closing Thought

The leader who corrects with gentleness is not less serious about the problem. They are more serious about what happens next.
