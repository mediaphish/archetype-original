## Scripture

**Matthew 26:36–39**

---

## Reflection

Jesus enters the moment where cost becomes immediate.

There is no distance between what is coming and what is felt. He does not deny the weight of it.

Servant leadership does not ignore difficulty. It faces it directly.

Jesus expresses the tension clearly. There is desire for another way, yet there is full alignment with the will of the Father.

This is not passive acceptance. It is deliberate surrender.

Leadership often reaches moments where the right path is also the hardest one.

Jesus shows that clarity does not remove struggle. It anchors obedience within it.

---

## Practical Application

- Acknowledge a decision that carries weight.
- Choose alignment over avoidance.
- Act with clarity even when it is difficult.

---

## Takeaways

- Surrender aligns leadership with purpose.
- Clarity does not remove difficulty.

---

## Closing Thought

Strong leadership moves forward even when the cost is clear.
